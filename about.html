About NorthPlate

Free, open-source Canadian vehicle intelligence — no login, no ads, no paywalls. Last updated: March 2026.

📋 What NorthPlate Is

NorthPlate is a free, browser-based tool for Canadians researching their next vehicle purchase. It aggregates publicly available data from government and industry sources into a single, easy-to-use interface.

Every number is traceable to an official source. No affiliate links. No dealer referrals. No advertising.

  • Browse and filter 52+ Canadian 2026 model vehicles
  • Compare up to 4 vehicles side-by-side with a full spec table
  • Estimate total cost of ownership for all 13 provinces and territories
  • Winter Scores that quantify cold-weather capability objectively
  • Cold EV range estimates based on the CAA −20°C study
  • Export filtered results to CSV

🚫 What NorthPlate Is Not

  • Not financial advice. TCO estimates are models, not guarantees. Consult a financial advisor for major purchase decisions.
  • Not a dealership tool. MSRPs shown are base MSRP. Actual transaction prices vary by dealer, region, and negotiation.
  • Not real-time. Data reflects NRCan's 2026 Fuel Consumption Guide published in early 2026. Prices may have changed.
  • Not affiliated. NorthPlate has no relationship with any automaker, dealer, insurer, or government agency.
  • Not exhastive. Stage 1 covers approximately 52 popular 2026 Canadian models. More will be added in later stages.

❄ Winter Score Methodology

The Winter Score (0–100) quantifies a vehicle's suitability for Canadian winter driving across four objective dimensions:

Winter Score = Drivetrain (40) + Ground Clearance (30) + Cold EV Range (20) + Body Style (10) Drivetrain (max 40 pts): AWD or 4WD → 40 pts (best traction control in snow) FWD → 20 pts (adequate, front weight over drive wheels) RWD → 5 pts (most challenging in snow, no front-engine weight benefit) Ground Clearance (max 30 pts): ≥ 200 mm → 30 pts (clears typical Canadian snow drifts) 170–199 mm → 20 pts (marginal in deep snow) < 170 mm → 10 pts (risks high-centering in unplowed conditions) Cold EV Range at −20°C (max 20 pts) — BEV only: ≥ 300 km → 20 pts (comfortable margin for most Canadian routes) 200–299 km → 15 pts (adequate for urban/suburban use) 100–199 km → 8 pts (limited — rural Canada may be challenging) < 100 km → 0 pts (range anxiety concern in cold climates) ICE / Hybrid / PHEV → 20 pts automatically (no range penalty) Body Style (max 10 pts): Truck or SUV → 10 pts (higher ground clearance, more mass) Car → 5 pts (lower clearance, lighter) Tiers: 80–100 → Excellent ❄ (navy badge) 65–79 → Good ❄ (teal badge) 40–64 → Fair ⚠ (amber badge) 0–39 → Poor ✕ (red badge)

Limitations: The Winter Score does not account for winter tire selection (the single biggest winter safety factor), driver experience, or specific regional conditions. Always install proper winter tires regardless of drivetrain.

🔋 Cold EV Range

EV batteries lose significant capacity in cold weather due to chemical slowdown and cabin heating loads.

Cold Range = Rated Range × 0.60 Source: CAA EV Cold-Weather Study (−20°C)

The CAA study found that at −20°C, EVs lose approximately 40% of their rated range on average. This varies by model, driving style, and pre-conditioning, but 0.60 is the consensus conservative estimate used here.

PHEV vehicles have a rated EV range but fall back to gasoline, so range anxiety is less critical. PHEVs show their EV range for reference.

💰 TCO Model

Total Cost = Net Purchase + Running Costs + Finance Cost Net Purchase = MSRP − EV Rebate (provincial + federal iZEV) Running Costs (per year, × ownership period): Fuel / Energy Cost + Insurance (IBC provincial benchmark) + Maintenance (fuel-type estimate) + Registration (MSRP-scaled estimate) Maintenance annual estimates: Gasoline → $1,800/yr Diesel → $2,000/yr Hybrid → $1,400/yr PHEV → $1,200/yr BEV → $900/yr (no oil, fewer brake services, fewer parts) Depreciation model: Year 1: 20% of MSRP Years 2–3: 15% of remaining value/year Years 4+: 10% of remaining value/year Finance cost (simple interest estimate): Interest ≈ (Principal ÷ 2) × Rate × Years (Average outstanding balance method — conservative) Fuel cost: Gasoline/Diesel: (km/100) × L/100km × provincial $/L Hybrid: (km/100) × L/100km × provincial $/L PHEV: 60% electric + 40% gasoline (real-world split) BEV: (km/100) × ~20 kWh/100km × $0.14/kWh (national avg)

⚖ Weighted Scoring

The personal scoring tool on the Compare page lets you assign importance weights (0–10) to five dimensions:

  • Purchase Price — normalised MSRP (lower is better)
  • Fuel Economy — normalised combined L/100km or EV range (lower consumption is better)
  • Winter Capability — Winter Score (higher is better)
  • EV Range — normalised rated range in km (higher is better)
  • Environmental Rating — NRCan CO₂ rating out of 10 (higher is better)
For each dimension d: normalisedScore_d = (value − min) / (max − min) × 100 (inverted for "lower is better" dimensions) weightedSum = Σ(normalisedScore_d × weight_d) finalScore = weightedSum / totalWeight (rounded to integer)

Scores of 50 mean the vehicle is average on that dimension relative to the compared set.
