About NorthPlate

Free, open-source Canadian vehicle intelligence — no login, no ads, no paywalls. Data current as of 2026.

What NorthPlate Is

NorthPlate is a free, browser-based tool for Canadians researching their next vehicle purchase. It aggregates publicly available data from government and industry sources into a single, easy-to-use interface — with no login, no ads, and no affiliate links.

Every number is traceable to an official source. The goal is straightforward: give Canadian drivers the context they need to make informed decisions, without the noise.

  • Browse and filter 870+ 2026 Canadian vehicles from the NRCan Fuel Consumption Guide
  • Compare up to 4 vehicles side-by-side with a full specification table
  • Estimate running costs including fuel, insurance, maintenance, and depreciation
  • Winter Readiness ratings that explain cold-weather capability clearly and transparently
  • Cold EV range estimates for all battery-electric vehicles at −20°C
  • CO₂ emissions in g/km (calculated from fuel consumption data)
  • Specs displayed in both metric and imperial units
  • Cost Analysis page including fuel cost modelling and tire cost calculator
  • Tire catalogue with size and brand options across all categories
  • Export any filtered vehicle list to CSV

Scope: NorthPlate covers 2026 Canadian market vehicles sourced from NRCan's annual Fuel Consumption Guide. MSRP data is base manufacturer suggested retail price and varies by trim, dealer, and region. Not affiliated with any manufacturer, dealer, insurer, or government agency.

❄ Winter Readiness Methodology

Winter Readiness rates a vehicle's suitability for Canadian winter driving across three objective, clearly-explained dimensions. Rather than a single opaque score, each factor is explained in plain language on the vehicle detail page.

Drivetrain

AWD / 4WD → Excellent All four wheels receive engine power. Traction is distributed dynamically, significantly reducing wheelspin on snow and ice. Best for winter driving. FWD → Good Engine weight sits over the drive wheels. Adequate for most urban and suburban Canadian winter conditions. Winter tires improve performance markedly. RWD → Poor Engine weight is over the non-driven wheels. Most demanding in winter; requires greater driver skill and winter tires are especially critical.

Ground Clearance

≥ 200 mm → High Clears deep snowdrifts and unplowed roads without risk of high-centering. Well-suited to rural, cottage-country, and severe winter conditions. 170–199 mm → Adequate Handles maintained winter roads reliably. Use caution in deep, unplowed snow or when crossing high snowbanks. 140–169 mm → Moderate Suitable for regularly plowed urban roads. Avoid deep snow accumulations. < 140 mm → Low Best confined to cleared urban roads. Not recommended for unplowed conditions or heavy snowfall.

Cold EV Range — Battery-Electric Vehicles Only

≥ 300 km → Comfortable Suitable for most Canadian inter-city winter routes, including cross-regional drives in most provinces. 200–299 km → Adequate Good for urban and suburban use. Plan charging stops for longer winter inter-city trips. 100–199 km → Limited Urban commutes with reliable charging access. Pre-conditioning the battery before departure is strongly recommended. < 100 km → Very Limited Short urban trips only, with guaranteed charging access.

Overall Winter Capability

Excellent — AWD/4WD + High clearance Good — AWD/4WD with moderate clearance, OR FWD with high clearance Fair — FWD with moderate clearance, or RWD with high clearance Poor — RWD with low clearance

Important note: Winter tires are the single most impactful factor in winter safety — more than drivetrain type. The Winter Readiness rating does not account for tire selection, driver experience, or specific regional conditions. Winter tires are legally required in British Columbia and strongly recommended everywhere in Canada.

🔋 Cold EV Range

EV batteries lose significant capacity in cold weather due to electrochemical slowdown and the energy load of cabin heating.

Cold Range = Rated Range × 0.60 Source: CAA EV Cold-Weather Study (tested at −20°C)

The CAA study found an average 40% range loss at −20°C. This varies by model, speed, cabin heating use, and whether the battery was pre-conditioned, but 0.60 is the conservative consensus estimate used here.

PHEV vehicles show their electric range for reference but fall back to gasoline, so cold-weather range anxiety is less of a concern for PHEVs.

🌿 CO₂ Emissions

CO₂ emissions are displayed in grams per kilometre (g/km), estimated from the vehicle's combined fuel consumption using NRCan's standard emission factors:

CO₂ (g/km) = Combined L/100km × Emission Factor Emission factors (NRCan standard): Gasoline: 22.89 g CO₂ per unit (L/100km) Diesel: 26.41 g CO₂ per unit (L/100km) BEV: 0 g/km tailpipe emissions Note: These are estimated tailpipe emissions only. Lifecycle emissions (manufacturing, electricity generation) are not included.

💰 Cost Analysis Model

Annual Running Cost = Fuel + Insurance + Maintenance + Registration Fuel cost (annual): Gasoline/Diesel/Hybrid: (km/100) × L/100km × provincial $/L PHEV: 60% electric + 40% gasoline (typical real-world split) BEV: (km/100) × ~20 kWh/100km × $0.14/kWh (national avg) Maintenance estimates (annual): Gasoline → $1,800 Diesel → $2,000 Hybrid → $1,400 PHEV → $1,200 BEV → $900 (no oil changes, fewer brake services) Depreciation: Year 1: 20% of purchase price Years 2–3: 15% of remaining value per year Years 4+: 10% of remaining value per year Insurance: IBC provincial benchmark averages (2024–2025) Registration: MSRP-scaled estimate ($150–$350/yr depending on vehicle value) EV rebates applied where eligible: Federal iZEV: up to $5,000 (BEV/PHEV) Provincial programs vary — see fuel.js for current values by province

🔧 Tire Costs

Tire cost estimates in the Cost Analysis page are based on the Tires catalogue, which lists current representative retail prices for popular Canadian tire models across all categories (winter, all-season, all-weather, summer).

Replacement intervals are estimates based on manufacturer treadwear ratings and typical Canadian driving conditions. Actual longevity depends on driving style, road conditions, and seasonal rotation practice.

Prices are in Canadian dollars and are updated quarterly. Always confirm current pricing with a local retailer.

⚖ Personal Scoring (Compare Page)

The Compare page allows you to assign personal importance weights (0–10) to five dimensions and calculates a weighted score across the vehicles you are comparing:

  • Purchase Price — normalised MSRP across compared vehicles (lower is better)
  • Fuel Economy — combined L/100km or EV range (lower consumption is better)
  • Winter Capability — overall Winter Readiness tier (higher is better)
  • EV Range — rated range in km (higher is better)
  • Environmental Rating — NRCan CO₂ rating out of 10 (higher is better)
For each dimension: normalisedScore = (value − min) / (max − min) × 100 (inverted for "lower is better" dimensions) finalScore = Σ(normalisedScore × weight) / totalWeight A score of 50 means the vehicle is exactly average on that dimension relative to the set of vehicles you are comparing.
